Ethics and Computer Ethics

Lecture Summaries

  • Lecture 1: Ethics and Computer Ethics

    Introduction to ethics, computer ethics, and different perspectives on technology and society.

  • Lecture 2: Ethical Theories and Perspectives

    Examination of various schools of ethical thinking and philosophical perspectives that guide moral behavior.

  • Lecture 3: The Digital Divide

    Exploration of the relationship between technology and society, focusing on digital divide and reasons for digital exclusion.

  • Lecture 4: The Role of the Internet in Democracy

    Examination of the interaction between technology and democracy, focusing on access to information.

  • Lecture 5: Algorithms and Algorithmic Bias

    Understanding algorithms, their influence on society, and the ethical concerns of algorithmic bias.

  • Lecture 6: Datafication and Data Justice

    Exploration of how human behavior is transformed into data points and the ethical implications for fairness and justice.

  • Lecture 7: Privacy and Data Protection

    Overview of privacy concepts, data protection laws, and technologies that encroach on privacy.

  • Lecture 8: Computer & Related Crimes

    Examination of cybercrimes, legal frameworks, and regulatory approaches to computer-related offenses.

  • Lecture 9: Professionalism and the Computing Profession

    Analysis of what constitutes a profession and whether computing meets these criteria, including issues of representation.

  • Lecture 10: Research Methods in Computing

    Overview of research methodologies, strategies, and data collection methods in computing research.
